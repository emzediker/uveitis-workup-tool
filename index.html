Plan

Recommended Management Plan:
  • Topical steroid: Prednisolone acetate 1% OD.
  • Dosage / Sig: 1 drop every hour while awake.
  • Cycloplegic: Homatropine 5% OD BID.
  • IOP monitoring: Check IOP at each follow-up; treat steroid response if present.
  • Referral / Co-management: Refer to primary care or pulmonology for systemic sarcoidosis evaluation and management.
  • Follow-Up: Return in 2–3 days to confirm improvement in AC cells/flare and assess IOP.

Patient Explanation

How would you explain this diagnosis and treatment to the patient in layperson terms? (Must include diagnosis and etiology.)

Suggested Explanation:

“You have inflammation inside your right eye called uveitis. Based on how your eye looks and your test results, this may be related to a condition called sarcoidosis, which can cause inflammation in different parts of the body, including the lungs and eyes.

We’re treating the eye inflammation with a strong anti-inflammatory eye drop, and we’re using a second drop to rest the iris, which helps with pain and helps prevent internal sticking or scarring.

Because this may be connected to inflammation elsewhere in your body, we’re also referring you for additional medical evaluation. I want to see you back in 2–3 days to make sure the inflammation is improving and to monitor your eye pressure.

If you notice worsening vision, increasing pain, new floaters, flashing lights, or a sudden change in vision, contact us right away or seek urgent care.”

Please list additional tests you would order at this time, along with the indication for each.

Ancillary Test Results:

    Indication: Rule out hypertensive optic disc edema / bilateral neuroretinitis pattern.

    Indication: Assess for macular edema and confirm star-related exudation pattern.

    Indication: Rule out syphilis (common “great masquerader” cause of uveitis).
    Result: Negative.

Plan

Recommended Management Plan:
  • Antibiotic therapy: Doxycycline 100 mg PO.
  • Dosage / Sig: Take 1 capsule by mouth twice daily for 14–21 days (coordinate duration with PCP/infectious disease).
  • Inflammation control: Prednisolone acetate 1% OS QID after systemic antibiotic is initiated (if ocular inflammation warrants).
  • Referral: Refer to primary care / infectious disease for systemic Lyme management within 1 week.
  • Follow-Up: Return in 3–5 days to reassess vision, disc edema, and inflammation; repeat OCT as indicated.

Patient Explanation

How would you explain this diagnosis and treatment to the patient in layperson terms? (Must include diagnosis and etiology.)

Suggested Explanation:

“You have inflammation in the back part of your left eye, and it’s affecting the optic nerve and retina, which is why you’re noticing blur and floaters. Based on your symptoms and the blood testing, this appears related to Lyme disease, which is an infection spread by ticks.

The most important treatment is the oral antibiotic to address the underlying infection. Once that’s started, we may also use anti-inflammatory eye drops to calm the eye inflammation, depending on how active it is.

I want to see you back in 3–5 days to make sure the eye inflammation is improving and that your vision and optic nerve findings are stabilizing. If you notice worsening vision, new flashes of light, a curtain over your vision, increasing pain, or rapidly increasing floaters, contact us right away or seek urgent care.”

- Pain with eye movement
- Decreased color vision OD
- Mild headache
- No trauma
- No known autoimmune disease

Visual Acuity:
OD: 20/80
OS: 20/20

Pupils:
Right RAPD present

Anterior Segment:
OU: Normal

Posterior Segment:
OD: Mild optic disc edema
OS: Normal

Fundus photo (OD)

Ancillary Testing

  • MRI brain and orbits with contrast:
    Indication: Evaluate optic nerve inflammation and demyelinating disease.
    Result: Optic nerve enhancement OD with periventricular white matter lesions.
  • ESR / CRP:
    Indication: Rule out arteritic or systemic inflammatory optic neuropathy.
    Result: Within normal limits.

Diagnosis

Correct Diagnosis: Optic neuritis (demyelinating), OD

Plan

  • Referral: Urgent neurology referral within 24–72 hours.
  • Imaging: MRI brain/orbits already obtained.
  • Treatment: IV corticosteroids per neurology if indicated.
  • Follow-Up: 1 week, then coordinated care.
